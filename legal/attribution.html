Standing on the Shoulders of Giants

Recursive.eco exists because of the countless open source contributors, Creative Commons creators, and visionary thinkers who chose to share their work openly. This page honors those contributions and explains how we build upon them to create something new while preserving the commons.

Core Philosophical Foundations

TB

Two Bits by Christopher M. Kelty

Work: Two Bits: The Cultural Significance of Free Software

License: CC BY-NC-SA 3.0

How we use it: Theoretical framework for recursive publics

Key concept: "A public that maintains the means of its own existence"

Kelty's work provides the conceptual foundation for Recursive.eco's approach to community building. His notion of a "recursive public" - a community vitally concerned with maintaining and modifying the means of its own existence - directly influences our governance model and technical architecture.

How to Be Added

If you've contributed to Recursive.eco or if we've built upon your CC-licensed work and you want to be featured here:

  1. Open an issue on our GitHub
  2. Email pp@playfulprocess.com
  3. Include: Your work, license type, and preferred attribution

Our Commitment

Everything Recursive.eco creates is licensed under Creative Commons BY-SA 4.0

This ensures our work can be freely:

  • Shared
  • Adapted
  • Built upon
  • Improved

With the requirement that:

  • Attribution is given
  • Same license is used for derivatives

Why Creative Commons BY-SA 4.0?

As explained in our blog post "Jongu as a Recursive Public", we believe that ShareAlike provisions ensure that improvements to our work remain open for everyone. This creates a sustainable commons where collective improvement benefits all participants.

Community Contribution Guidelines

We transform the collective by transforming ourselves - including how we share knowledge

Default License: CC BY-SA 4.0

By contributing to Recursive.eco, you agree that your contributions will be licensed under Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 unless you explicitly specify otherwise.

You Can

  • Be attributed for your work
  • Share contributions elsewhere
  • Build upon them independently
  • Use them commercially

We Can

  • Include contributions in Recursive.eco
  • Modify and adapt them
  • Share them with community
  • Build upon them

Everyone Must

  • Give you attribution
  • Share derivatives under same license
  • Indicate if changes were made

The Recursive Commitment

Contributing to Recursive.eco means participating in a recursive public where:

1

Your work remains open for others to build upon

2

Improvements flow back to the community

3

Attribution creates genealogy of ideas

4

No one can close what you've opened

By contributing, you join a recursive public building tools for human transformation in the age of AI.
